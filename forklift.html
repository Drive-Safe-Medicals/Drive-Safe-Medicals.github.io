Forklift Driver Medicals

To The Health and Safety Executive (HSE) advises that all workplace transport operators should have
an assessment for forklift operator fitness before and during their employment. The standard practice
is to screen transport operators at five-yearly intervals from the age 45.

The group 2 licence is renewable five-yearly from the age 45. If the employee has both a workplace
transport operator and a group 2 licence to be renewed, we can schedule both assessments within
the same examination.

It is likely that the illness can affect the worker’s fitness. If an employee has been away for a short
absence or absent from work for more than a month, HSE advises all workplace transport operators
to have a formal medical assessment when they return to work. Under these circumstances, we will
assess whether the individual is fit to operate workplace transport.

Our accredited GMC registered doctors will assess your fitness for workplace transport. Once you
have successfully passed your medical assessment, we will provide a fitness certification within
24 hours of your medical assessment.

THE STANDARD FORKLIFT DRIVER MEDICAL INVOLVES:

  • A series of questions about your medical history
  • Height and weight measurement
  • Blood pressure check
  • Eye test

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER I HAVE MADE A BOOKING?

Once you have successfully booked and paid for your appointment. You will be sent a confirmation

WHAT SHOULD I BRING TO THE APPOINTMENT?

  1. Photo ID – Driving licence/Passport
  2. Your driving glasses and your glasses prescription if you wear glasses (not contact lenses)
    to meet the eyesight standard needed for driving
  3. Your medication prescription
